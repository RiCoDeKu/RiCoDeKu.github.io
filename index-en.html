Researches

Undergraduate Research: VLM-based Zero-shot Dense Video Captioning of Instructional Videos Using Hand-Centric Object Context Against the backdrop of labor shortages in manufacturing and service industries, there is a growing need to automate skill transfer and manual creation from work videos. Dense Video Captioning (DVC), which generates both work segments and descriptions, is attracting attention as a core technology. However, conventional DVC methods require large amounts of supervised data, which limits practical use. Zero-shot methods using Vision-Language Models (VLMs) can also produce incorrect captions or over-segment scenes when fine hand motions and nearby objects are difficult to see. This research proposes a zero-shot DVC method that aggregates object-class information around the hands over time and adds it to the VLM prompt as semantic context. Experiments on cooking and assembly work video datasets confirmed better caption quality and scene segmentation accuracy than comparison methods. Open presentation page
Master's Research: Motion Comic Generation by Adapting Video Generation Models to Manga Panel Images Diffusion-based Image-to-Video (I2V) technology is making it possible to generate video from still images. In this research, I evaluate whether pretrained I2V models can be applied to manga panel images and examine how manga-specific expressions, such as speech balloons, sound-effect text, and speed lines, affect I2V generation. Removing manga-specific expressions reduced unnatural motion, and letterbox padding improved movement expressions that use speed lines.
